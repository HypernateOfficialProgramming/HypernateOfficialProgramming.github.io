The Pandemic - A school essay

During The 11th of March in the year 2020, The global COVID-19 outbreak was declared as a pandemic by World Health Organization. Everyone I saw were wearing masks to shield themselves from the incoming invasion, a strain of viruses known as SARS-CoV-2.

People were dying left and right as many caught the infection, failling into terminal illness.

Almost all airports were closed due to this disaster, and the hoarding of resources became increasingly more common.

In some areas, hotels were converted into quarantine zones for the virus.

I never recognized the magnitude of this situation, until February 2022.

Brink of Collapse

My mother was hit with the sickness, and it spread on to me, then finally my father.

For a moment, everything felt normal. It felt to us like an ordinary flu, nothing seemed off.

However, once my father finally caught it... something horrible happened to him.

He caught pneumonia, a lung infection that causes inflammation and fluid in the alveoli (the air sacs within the lungs), making it extremely difficult to breathe.

Safe Zone

My father was taken to the hospital for 2 weeks. He had occasional video calls with us in his hospital bed, and... it was slightly worrying.

What would happen? Would he make it? Would everything return to normal?

Thankfully, a miracle happened. He was healing. He got less and less sick until he fully recovered.

And even after all of this, the hospital payed for it. The government payed for each of the patients affected by SARS-CoV-2, making sure it was completely free for everyone.

The Grand Cure

Finally, a few months after the event, a cure was developed. We had two progressive shots, a primary and, after 6 months, a booster shot.

When we got to the hospital, we went to get those injections, and once the needle pierced through my skin… unlike this video, there were no cries for help, no painful stings—just a single weird feeling on my shoulder, and… nothing.

It felt strange. Weird. I couldn’t comprehend it.

And just like that, our worries, pain, and suffering all ended. We were immune now.
